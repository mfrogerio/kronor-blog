Sweden Arrival Checklist

28 things to do in your first 90 days

PRE-ARRIVAL — 2 weeks before
  • Open a multi-currency account to receive salary without losing money
  • Get a travel eSIM for internet from the moment you land
  • Research health insurance for the gap before Swedish coverage begins
  • Book accommodation for your first week
  • Gather key documents: passport, employment contract, rental agreement
WEEK 1 — Immediate priorities
  • Register with the migration authority and get your confirmation number
  • Register your address with the tax office (folkbokföring)
  • Apply for your personnummer (personal identity number)
  • Get a Swedish SIM card
  • Open a bank account
  • Find temporary housing
  • Join expat groups online
WEEK 2–4 — While you wait
  • Follow up on your personnummer application
  • Join the housing queue (bostadskö)
  • Get BankID once personnummer arrives
  • Set up mobile payments (Swish)
  • Download your digital mailbox app for official letters (Kivra)
  • Register for unemployment insurance (A-kassa)
  • Join a union (facket) if employed
MONTH 2 — Settling in
  • Confirm your personnummer is approved
  • Get home insurance (hemförsäkring — essential, not optional)
  • Set up electricity and internet contracts
  • Open a tax-efficient investment account (ISK)
  • Research childcare options if relevant — waitlists are long
MONTH 3 — Building your life
  • Start Swedish language classes — SFI is free for residents
  • Join a gym, club, or hobby group
  • Review your salary setup with HR
  • Set up automatic bill payments (autogiro)
